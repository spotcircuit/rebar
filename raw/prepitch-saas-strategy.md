### Detailed Competitor Profiles

#### 1. Second Nature AI
- **What they do:** AI avatar-based sales roleplay training that reduces onboarding time by 30%.
- **Key features:** Static-to-realistic avatars, multi-stakeholder simulations, text-based chat roleplays, SCORM/LTI LMS integration, comprehensive analytics.
- **Strengths:** Best avatar quality in the space; deep LMS integrations (Workday, Cornerstone); proven enterprise results (7x time spent role-playing).
- **Weaknesses:** Enterprise-only pricing excludes SMBs and individuals; sales-only vertical; no VC/PE/interview modes.
- **How PrePitch compares:** PrePitch offers broader verticals (sales + VC + PE + interview) and can serve individuals/startups, not just enterprises.

#### 2. Hyperbound (YC-backed)
- **What they do:** AI cold calling practice trained on 2M+ hours of real B2B sales data.
- **Key features:** ICP-to-persona in <2 min, AI scorecards for any methodology (MEDDIC, BANT, etc.), Gong/Salesloft/Chorus integration, bitesized roleplays.
- **Strengths:** Deep sales methodology alignment; CRM integration surfaces real skill gaps; fast persona creation; strong enterprise traction (Vanta: 60% ramp reduction).
- **Weaknesses:** Cold-call focused only; no VC/PE/interview verticals; no avatar; enterprise pricing only.
- **How PrePitch compares:** PrePitch covers 4 verticals vs. Hyperbound's 1; PrePitch has avatar capability; PrePitch serves founders and individuals too.

#### 3. Quantified
- **What they do:** AI roleplay + certification for highly regulated industries (pharma, medtech, finance).
- **Key features:** Auto-builds simulations from training content/CRM/LMS data; certification tracking; compliance-ready; launches programs in hours.
- **Strengths:** Deep regulatory compliance focus; trusted by Novartis, Bayer, Sanofi; auto-ingests training materials.
- **Weaknesses:** Narrow vertical focus (pharma/medtech); enterprise-only; complex setup; no individual user option.
- **How PrePitch compares:** PrePitch is more accessible and broader; Quantified owns regulated industries. PrePitch's PE vertical could overlap with Quantified's finance segment.

#### 4. Pitch Monster
- **What they do:** AI sales roleplay with gamification (leaderboards, scoring, incentives).
- **Key features:** 48 ready-to-use scenarios, custom scenario builder, API/CRM integrations, gamification/leaderboards, team competitions.
- **Strengths:** Strong gamification angle; large scenario library; good for team motivation.
- **Weaknesses:** Minimum 10-user requirement excludes individuals; sales-only; no voice avatar; dated UI compared to newer entrants.
- **How PrePitch compares:** PrePitch has similar features (37 templates, leaderboard, team dashboard) plus voice mode, avatar, and 4 verticals. PrePitch also serves individual users.

#### 5. Yoodli (Google-backed)
- **What they do:** AI speech and communication coaching with deep analytics on delivery (filler words, pacing, eye contact).
- **Key features:** Free tier, speech analytics (pace, tone, clarity, filler words, eye contact), body language analysis via webcam, progress tracking.
- **Strengths:** Best freemium entry point ($0-$20/mo); excellent speech delivery analytics; good for interview prep; Google backing.
- **Weaknesses:** Not a sales roleplay tool -- no buyer personas, no objection handling, no deal simulation; limited to speech coaching; no vertical depth.
- **How PrePitch compares:** PrePitch has deep roleplay simulation with buyer personas and scoring across business verticals. Yoodli excels at speech delivery mechanics that PrePitch could incorporate.

#### 6. Gong / Chorus (Adjacent)
- **What they do:** Post-call conversation intelligence -- record, transcribe, and analyze real sales calls.
- **Pricing:** Gong: $1,600/user/year + $5K-50K platform fee. Chorus: $8K/year base + $1,200/seat.
- **Strengths:** Market leaders in conversation intelligence; real call data; proven ROI at enterprise scale.
- **Weaknesses:** Analyze real calls, don't simulate practice; expensive; no practice/training mode; no pre-call preparation.
- **How PrePitch compares:** Complementary rather than competitive. PrePitch = practice before the call; Gong/Chorus = analyze after the call. Integration opportunity: import Gong insights to create targeted PrePitch practice scenarios.

#### 7. Brevity
- **What they do:** Voice-first AI sales roleplay -- reps practice via phone call, no app or login needed.
- **Key features:** Phone-based roleplay (zero friction), Ella Quint voice assistant for scenario building, performance dashboards, persona customization by industry/stage/objection.
- **Strengths:** Brilliantly simple UX -- just call a number; zero onboarding friction; good for field sales.
- **Weaknesses:** Phone-only limits rich media (no avatar, no visual cues); no VC/PE/interview verticals; limited analytics compared to web-based tools.
- **How PrePitch compares:** PrePitch offers richer experience (text + voice + avatar + analytics); Brevity's phone-first approach is worth studying for frictionless access.

#### 8. Poised
- **What they do:** Real-time AI coach that monitors video calls and provides private communication feedback.
- **Key features:** Live meeting coaching, filler word tracking, pacing/tone analysis, meeting notes automation, works with Zoom/Teams/Meet/Webex.
- **Strengths:** Works during real meetings (not just practice); strong privacy architecture (local processing); good integrations.
- **Weaknesses:** Passive coach (observes, doesn't simulate); no roleplay capability; no buyer personas; limited to communication style.
- **How PrePitch compares:** Different use case. Poised = real-time meeting assistant; PrePitch = active practice simulator. Could be complementary.

#### 9. Outdoo
- **What they do:** AI roleplay with CRM-synced buyer personas and AI voice cloning of real prospects.
- **Key features:** CRM-pulled buyer profiles, voice cloning of ICPs, deal-stage context in roleplays, unified scoring across practice + live calls, 120+ integrations.
- **Strengths:** Best CRM integration in the space; voice cloning is unique and compelling; closed-loop coaching (practice data informs live coaching).
- **Weaknesses:** Complex enterprise setup; no individual user tier; sales-only; usage-based pricing can be unpredictable.
- **How PrePitch compares:** Outdoo's CRM sync and voice cloning are features PrePitch should study. PrePitch's multi-vertical approach and accessibility (individuals + teams) differentiate it.

#### 10. Other Notable Tools
- **Allego:** Enterprise enablement with AI roleplays, video exercises, call analysis. Heavy enterprise focus.
- **FullyRamped:** Newer entrant focused on onboarding acceleration and cold call training for SMB/mid-market.
- **Awarathon:** Video roleplay platform popular in APAC markets.
- **Tough Tongue AI:** Specialized LLM-powered buyer personas with custom objection matrices.
- **Final Round AI / Revarta / Skillora:** Interview-focused AI practice tools ($29-49/mo for individuals).

### 3. Pricing Model

**Recommendation: Freemium + per-seat tiers with vertical add-ons.**

Based on competitive analysis, the market breaks into three bands:
- **Individual tools:** $0-20/mo (Yoodli, Poised)
- **Team tools:** $15-50/user/mo (Pitch Monster, SalesHood, Mindtickle)
- **Enterprise platforms:** $100-250/user/mo (Gong, Second Nature, Quantified)

**Proposed PrePitch Pricing:**

| Plan | Price | Includes | Target |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Free** | $0 | 3 sessions/month, 1 vertical (sales or interview), text-only roleplay, basic debrief (5 criteria) | Individual trial, job seekers |
| **Starter** | $19/mo ($15/mo annual) | 20 sessions/month, all 4 verticals, voice mode, full 44-criteria debrief, session history, analytics | Solo founders, individual reps, job seekers |
| **Pro** | $39/mo ($29/mo annual) | Unlimited sessions, all verticals, voice + avatar, custom persona builder, objection library, shared links, export | Power users, startup teams (<5) |
| **Team** | $29/user/mo ($22/user/mo annual) | Everything in Pro + team dashboard, leaderboard, manager analytics, certification tracking, 5-seat minimum | Sales teams, recruiting firms |
| **Enterprise** | Custom (starting $45/user/mo) | Everything in Team + SSO/SAML, CRM integration, LMS export, custom knowledge bases, SLA, dedicated support | Enterprise sales orgs, PE firms |

**Why this works:**
- Free tier converts individual users (Yoodli proves this model at $0/$8/$20)
- $19/mo Starter undercuts most competitors while covering 4 verticals
- $29/user/mo Team is competitive with Mindtickle ($30-50) and SalesHood ($50)
- Enterprise tier captures high-value PE/M&A clients willing to pay premium
- Usage-based voice/avatar minutes could be layered on top if needed

**Revenue model math:**
- 1,000 free users converting at 5% = 50 Starter ($950/mo)
- 200 Pro users = $7,800/mo
- 10 teams of 20 = $44,000/mo (at $22/user annual)
- 2 enterprise deals = $20,000+/mo
- **Target MRR at 12 months: $70K-100K**

### 7. Voice / Avatar Strategy for 2026

**Current state:** OpenAI Realtime (WebRTC) for voice, D-ID for avatar with 45s latency.

**Recommended approach:**

| Layer | Recommended Provider | Why | Latency |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Speech-to-Text** | Deepgram Nova-2 or OpenAI Whisper v3 | Fast, accurate, multilingual | <200ms |
| **LLM (roleplay brain)** | Claude Sonnet 4.5 (current) | Best roleplay quality, instruction following | 500-800ms first token |
| **Text-to-Speech** | ElevenLabs Flash v2.5 | Sub-75ms latency, emotional control, 32 voices | 75ms |
| **Avatar (Phase 1)** | Remove avatar, voice-only | 45s D-ID latency is unusable. Ship voice-first. | N/A |
| **Avatar (Phase 2)** | Tavus or HeyGen LiveAvatar | Tavus: sub-1s conversational latency, photorealistic. HeyGen LiveAvatar: new 2026 platform replacing Interactive Avatar. | <1s |
| **Avatar (Phase 3)** | Evaluate Anam.ai | 180ms average response time, purpose-built for conversational avatars. | 180ms |

**Strategic recommendation:**
1. **Immediate (P0):** Kill D-ID avatar. Ship voice-only mode with ElevenLabs Flash v2.5. Voice is the core experience; avatar is a bonus.
2. **Q3 2026:** Add Tavus avatar as premium feature (Pro+ plans). Their sub-1s latency makes conversational avatar viable.
3. **Q4 2026:** Evaluate Anam.ai for even lower latency (180ms). Consider offering avatar tier selection (basic voice, premium avatar).
4. **Ongoing:** Monitor ElevenLabs v3 for real-time support. When it ships real-time, consolidate TTS + avatar on ElevenLabs stack.

**Voice architecture target:**
```
User speaks -> Deepgram STT (200ms) -> Claude roleplay (600ms) -> ElevenLabs TTS (75ms) -> User hears response
Total: ~900ms end-to-end (feels conversational)

With avatar:
... -> ElevenLabs TTS (75ms) -> Tavus lip-sync (<1s) -> User sees + hears response  
Total: ~1.5s end-to-end (acceptable for avatar mode)
```

## Key Strategic Insights

1. **PE/M&A is the highest-value unserved niche.** No competitor touches it. Business owners preparing for $5M-500M exits will pay premium pricing and have zero alternatives. Partner with business brokers, EOS implementers, and M&A advisors.

2. **VC pitch practice has low competition and high virality.** PitchBob ($29.90) is the only tool, and it's basic. Founders share pitch practice with co-founders, advisors, and accelerator cohorts. Natural viral loop.

3. **Voice-first, avatar-second.** The avatar race is premature -- latency isn't solved yet (D-ID: 45s, best case Tavus: <1s). Ship excellent voice-only experience now. Add avatar as a premium feature when providers hit <500ms end-to-end.

4. **Individual users are the distribution advantage.** Every competitor requires 10+ seats. Individual founders and reps are an underserved entry point that feeds team/enterprise deals ("I used PrePitch to practice my pitch, my team should use it too").

5. **The 44-criteria debrief is the product moat.** Most competitors give 5-10 scoring dimensions. PrePitch's depth creates real improvement velocity that users can feel. Protect and expand this.

6. **Integration with Gong/Chorus creates a closed loop.** "Your last 5 real calls show weak discovery questions. Here's a practice session targeting that gap." This is where Outdoo and Hyperbound are heading. PrePitch should build toward this.
